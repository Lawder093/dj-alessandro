Chronological Signal

Timeline

2022

First Bedroom Mixes

A secondhand mixer, two borrowed decks, and a room small enough that the walls vibrated. The first recordings were made at night, when the building fell quiet. Nobody heard them. That was fine.

2023

Analog Experiments

Discovery of analog synthesis — the warm distortion of a 303 clone, the unstable pitch of a defective oscillator. The machines were broken in ways that couldn't be replicated digitally. Those imperfections became the sound.

2024

Blue Archive Sessions

Three nights inside an abandoned recording facility on the city's periphery. No clocks, no phones, no daylight. The results were archived and released six months later. People noticed.

2025

Underground Live Transmission

First live performances in small basement venues — spaces that held sixty people maximum and had no stage. Just a table, a system, and a crowd that stood very still. The recordings from that year became the Underground Transmission live document.

2026

Static Memory Protocol

The current album. Eight tracks of deliberate restraint — music that breathes through its own silences. Recorded during a winter of voluntary isolation. A transmission that is still ongoing.

Artist Statement

Manifesto

Electronic music did not begin in clubs. It began in bedrooms — in the friction between a machine and the person operating it, in the accidental beauty of a patch cable in the wrong socket, in the hiss of a cassette that had been recorded over too many times. That is where DJ Alessandro's music comes from: the accident. The error that turns out to be the song.

"The machine doesn't know what it means to be lonely. So I taught it. Now it plays that loneliness back to me and I have to decide if it's mine or its own."

The influence of Japanese retro-futurism runs through every record — not as aesthetic borrowing, but as a structural philosophy. The idea that technology and emotion are not opposites. That a synthesizer can grieve. That a drum machine can be patient. That the space between notes is as important as the notes themselves.

Blue Archive Sessions was about the city at 3AM. Analog Nostalgia was about the machines we loved before we understood them. Static Memory Protocol is about what remains when everything else is filtered away — the signal underneath the noise, the frequency that doesn't resolve.

This is not background music. It is not ambient. It is not designed to disappear. It is designed to transmit. Whether anyone receives it is, ultimately, not the point. The transmission is its own reason for existing.
